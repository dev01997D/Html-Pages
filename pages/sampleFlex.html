First article

The definite article is the word the. It limits the meaning of a noun to one particular thing.

For example, your friend might ask, “Are you going to the party this weekend?” The definite article

tells you that your friend is referring to a specific party that both of you know about.

Second Article: The Indefinite Article

The indefinite article takes two forms. It’s the word a when it precedes a word that begins with a

consonant. It’s the word an when it precedes a word that begins with a vowel.

Third Article: Exceptions: Choosing A or An

There are a few exceptions to the general rule of using a before words that start with consonants and

an before words that begin with vowels. The first letter of the word honor, for example, is a

consonant, but it’s unpronounced.
